# Revenue Stage Patterns

What successful founders actually did at each revenue milestone. Based on cross-referencing revenue data from StarterStory (1,000+ cases), IndieHackers (5,000+ revenue-transparent founders), YC Startup School, and MicroConf talks.

---

## Stage 0: Validation ($0 → $1K MRR)

**Core challenge**: Does anyone actually pay for this?

### The Winning Pattern

1. **Find one person with a painful problem**
2. **Offer to solve it manually** (service/consulting) or **pre-sell the solution** (before building)
3. **Charge immediately** — even if it's $50/month
4. **Obsessively iterate on their feedback**
5. **Find 3-5 more people like them**, repeat

### What Successful Founders Did

| Founder | Company | Revenue Path | Key Move |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pieter Levels | Nomad List ($1.5M ARR) | $0 → $500/mo in 2 months | Built in public on Twitter. Launched an ugly v1. Charged from day 1. |
| Nathan Barry | ConvertKit ($28M ARR) | $0 → $5K/mo in 6 months | Pre-sold an email course to his existing blog audience before writing a word. Earned $8K in 24 hours for a course that didn't exist. |
| Andrey Azimov | Sheet2Site ($100K ARR) | $0 → $1K/mo | Built in public. Shared every milestone on Twitter. Launched on Product Hunt with a working but ugly v1. |
| AJ | Carrd ($1.5M ARR) | $0 → $10K/mo in 2 years | One-page website builder. Charged $19/year initially. SEO as primary channel. No ads, no content marketing — just search. |

### Startup Diagnostic

- Has any stranger (not a friend) paid you yet? If not → you're pre-validation, not at $0→$1K.
- How many paying customers? <5 = focus on finding more like the first. >5 = start looking for patterns.
- What's the customer's "holy shit" moment? The exact point where they realize they need your product.
- Are you charging? If you have 0 paying users and are "building more features," you're building in the dark.

### Signal You're Stuck

- People say "great idea" but nobody pays → Not validated. Run more Mom Test conversations.
- People pay once but don't renew → Not recurring pain. Fix the core value prop before adding features.
- You're "almost ready to launch" for more than 2 weeks → Launch now. Ship ugly. Charge something.

## Stage 1: Product-Market Fit ($1K → $10K MRR)

**Core challenge**: Do users stay, pay more, and tell others?

### The Winning Pattern

1. **Pick ONE growth channel and master it** (not three)
2. **Nail pricing** — if nobody complains about price, you're too cheap
3. **Reduce churn before increasing growth** — leaky bucket wastes all marketing
4. **Build features based on what paying customers actually ask for** (not your roadmap)
5. **Create a "reason to tell a friend"** — a specific, memorable experience that triggers word of mouth

### What Successful Founders Did

| Founder | Company | Revenue Path | Key Move |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pieter Levels | Nomad List ($1.5M ARR) | $500 → $5K/mo | Added city cost-of-living calculator (viral feature). Shared transparent revenue publicly → "build in public" became his distribution channel. |
| Marie Prokopets | FYI ($200K ARR) | $0 → $15K/mo in 1 year | Niche: document collaboration for creative teams. Not "document tool" — "document tool for creative teams." Niche = lower competition, higher conversion. |
| Josh Ho | Referral Rock ($1.5M ARR) | $1K → $10K/mo | SEO as primary channel. Wrote 100+ long-form blog posts targeting specific referral marketing keywords. Took 18 months to see traffic, then compound growth kicked in. |
| Rob Walling | Drip (acquired) | $1K → $50K/mo | "Stair-step" approach: services → TinySeed fund → SaaS products. Drip was his 4th product. Each previous product taught him what to build next. |

### Startup Diagnostic

- What is your ONE growth channel? If you're trying 3+ channels → narrow to one.
- What is your monthly churn? <5% = healthy. 5-8% = yellow. >8% = fix churn first.
- What % of new customers come from word of mouth? If <20%, your product isn't remarkable enough to trigger referrals.
- Do you have "power users" who use your product differently than normal users? → They're showing you your real value prop.

### Signal You're Stuck

- Churn > revenue growth → You're replacing users, not growing. Fix the product, not the funnel.
- 3-month revenue plateau → Your current growth channel is maxed out. Time to add a second channel.
- "All my users ask for the same feature" for 3+ months without building it → You're ignoring your PMF signal.

## Stage 2: Scaling ($10K → $50K MRR)

**Core challenge**: Can you grow without breaking everything?

### The Winning Pattern

1. **Add outbound sales** (cold email, LinkedIn, partnerships) — not replacing organic, adding to it
2. **Optimize pricing** — raise prices, add annual plan, create an enterprise tier
3. **Upsell existing users** — it's 3-5x cheaper than acquiring new ones
4. **Start building process** — document SOPs for everything you do
5. **Hire your first specialist** — probably customer success or sales (not engineering)

### What Successful Founders Did

| Founder | Company | Revenue Path | Key Move |
|---|---|---|---|
| Nathan Barry | ConvertKit ($28M ARR) | $5K → $50K/mo | "Migration concierge" — personally migrated users from Mailchimp/Infusionsoft for free. Unscalable move that killed churn. |
| Patrick Campbell | ProfitWell (acquired) | $10K → $100K/mo | Content marketing engine. Published data-driven pricing research → became the authority. Inbound leads from content. |
| Hiten Shah | FYI, Crazy Egg, KISSmetrics | $10K → $100K/mo (multiple products) | Serial founder approach: build, grow to $10-50K, either sell or run. Pattern recognition from multiple attempts. |

### Startup Diagnostic

- Do you have annual pricing? Annual plan users churn 60-70% less than monthly.
- What's your expansion revenue? (existing customers paying more) — should be 20-30% of new revenue.
- How many support tickets per customer per month? If >5, product complexity is killing your growth.
- Do you have a documented process for: onboarding, support, bug triage, feature requests? If not, you can't delegate.

### Signal You're Stuck

- 3+ month plateau → current channel is saturated. Add one new channel — don't optimize the old one to death.
- Founder is answering all support tickets → you haven't hired. You are the bottleneck.
- Churn creep: churn slowly rising 0.5%/month → product complexity growing faster than value. Simplify.

## Stage 3: Team ($50K → $200K MRR)

**Core challenge**: Can you delegate without losing quality?

### The Winning Pattern

1. **Hire specialists** (not generalists) — head of sales, head of CS, marketing lead
2. **Add paid acquisition** — you now have LTV data and unit economics to justify ad spend
3. **Content marketing at scale** — not you writing, a team producing high-quality content
4. **Product-led growth (PLG)** — if your product has viral potential, now is the time to instrument it
5. **Build a leadership layer** — you should not be in every meeting, every decision

### What Successful Founders Did

| Founder | Company | Key Move |
|---|---|---|
| David Darmanin | Hotjar (acquired by Contentsquare) | Built an all-remote team from day 1. Process-first culture documented in public. Scaled to $40M ARR. |
| Amir Salihefendic | Todoist ($15M ARR) | Bootstrapped. Never raised VC. Built a remote team of 80+. Grew slowly for 10 years. |
| Des Traynor | Intercom ($200M+ ARR) | Went from customer support tool → customer communication platform. Expanding TAM as you grow. |

### Startup Diagnostic

- What % of decisions require you? If >30%, you haven't built a leadership layer.
- What's your employee churn? >20% annual = culture problem. <10% = healthy.
- Can you take a 2-week vacation and the company still grows? If not, you're the bottleneck.
- What's your revenue per employee? SaaS benchmark: $150K-250K/employee at this stage.

### Signal You're Stuck

- Founder is in every meeting → hire a COO or general manager.
- Growth feels "heavy" — same effort produces less result → you've hit the ceiling of your current GTM model.
- Team churn accelerating → you grew the team but not the culture/process. Invest in people ops.

## Stage 4: Engine ($200K → $1M+ MRR)

**Core challenge**: Can you compound growth across multiple channels?

### The Winning Pattern

1. **Multi-channel growth engine** — content + paid + outbound + partner + PLG all running simultaneously
2. **Product-line expansion** — add adjacent products for your existing customer base
3. **Affiliate and partner programs** — let others sell for you
4. **International expansion** — localize product and marketing for new geos
5. **Brand becomes a moat** — you are the default choice in your category

### What Successful Founders Did

| Company | Key Move |
|---|---|
| Zapier ($140M ARR) | API ecosystem. 5,000+ app integrations. Every integration is a distribution channel. |
| Canva ($2.3B ARR) | Freemium PLG → enterprise → team plans → print products. Multi-product company. |
| Atlassian ($4B+ ARR) | No sales team. Zero. Pure PLG + word of mouth for 15+ years. Multiple products (Jira, Confluence, Bitbucket, Trello). |

### Startup Diagnostic

- How many distinct, independent growth channels do you have? <3 = vulnerable. ≥4 = resilient.
- What % of revenue comes from your largest customer? >15% = concentration risk.
- What % of revenue comes from non-core products? 0% = single point of failure. >20% = healthy diversification.
- Do you have a partner/affiliate program? If not and you're B2B SaaS, you're leaving 20-30% of potential growth on the table.

### Signal You're Stuck

- Growth is "optimization only" — running A/B tests for 1% improvements → you're not finding new channels.
- All revenue from one product → single point of failure. Time to expand product line.
- Founder is CEO but still acting like early-stage founder → hire experienced executives. Your job has changed.

## Cross-Cutting Patterns (True at Every Stage)

### The "Charge from Day 1" Pattern

80% of StarterStory founders making $100K+/year charged from day 1 — or pre-sold before they had a product.
- The ones who waited "until it was ready" typically waited forever.
- Revenue is the only validation that can't be faked.

### The "One Channel Deep" Pattern

At every stage through $50K MRR, successful founders mastered ONE channel before adding a second.
- Trying 3+ channels at once = doing none well.
- The dominant channel at each stage: Validation = personal network → PMF = word of mouth → Scaling = content or outbound → Team = paid + content → Engine = all of them.

### The "Narrow Target" Pattern

Successful founders consistently defined their initial market more narrowly than you'd expect.
- "CRM for dog groomers with 2+ locations" → not "CRM for small businesses"
- "Email marketing for professional bloggers earning $10K+/mo" → not "email marketing"
- Narrow target = clear messaging = higher conversion = faster feedback = faster PMF

### The "Anti-Fragile Pricing" Pattern

Successful founders systematically raised prices as value increased:
- Validation: Price to learn (what are people willing to pay?)
- PMF: Price to survive (can you cover costs?)
- Scaling: Price to value (what is the product worth to the customer?)
- Engine: Price to expand (price anchoring, tiered plans, enterprise upsells)

### The "Default Alive" Pattern

Y Combinator's Paul Graham: "A startup is default alive if its current revenue growth rate, if sustained, will get it to profitability before it runs out of money."
- Successful bootstrapped founders aimed for profitability at $10K MRR.
- Successful VC-backed founders maintained 24+ months runway.
- Neither group counted on "the next round."
