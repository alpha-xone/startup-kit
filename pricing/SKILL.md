# Pricing · Pricing Lab

> **Price what it's worth, not what it costs.**

A structured pricing strategy guide distilled from real founder pricing experiments — including successes and failures. Built on Hormozi's Value Equation, the Pain-Ladder Pricing move from blueprint, and pricing data cross-referenced from StarterStory + IndieHackers.

## Core Principle

**Price is a function of perceived value, not production cost.** The difference between $100K ARR and $1M+ ARR companies is almost always pricing strategy, not product quality. $1M+ founders charge based on value delivered. $100K founders charge based on "what competitors charge."

### Three modes:

- **Value Assessment**: "How much should I charge?" → Walks through value equation + pain-ladder to find your price
- **Tier Design**: "How should I structure my plans?" → Tiering strategy matched to user segments
- **Price Audit**: "Should I raise my prices?" → Diagnostics based on churn data and willingness-to-pay signals

## The Pricing Framework

### Step 1: Value Calculation

Use Hormozi's Value Equation:

```
Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)
```

| Variable | What It Means | How to Increase It |
|---|---|---|
| **Dream Outcome** ↑ | What the customer actually wants | Make outcome more specific, vivid, desirable |
| **Perceived Likelihood** ↑ | How much they believe you can deliver | Case studies, guarantees, social proof, specificity |
| **Time Delay** ↓ | How long until they get the result | Faster onboarding, instant wins, Quick Start |
| **Effort & Sacrifice** ↓ | How hard the customer has to work | Done-for-you > done-with-you > DIY |

### Step 2: Pain-to-Price Mapping

| Pain Level | Value | Pricing Power | Pricing Strategy |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Existential** | ∞ | Charge anything | Per-seat premium, flat fee |
| **Major financial** (saves $10K+) | Very high | 10-30% of value | % of value, usage-based |
| **Productivity** (saves hours) | Moderate | Per-seat, $10-100/mo | Tiered by usage/features |
| **Convenience** (nice-to-have) | Low | $5-50/mo | Freemium → low-cost premium |

### Step 3: Tier Design Principles

| Tier | % of Users | Purpose | Feature Strategy | Data Backing |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Free** | 80-90% | Top-of-funnel, PLG | Core feature, limit usage | ConvertKit: free for first 1K subs → upgrade at 1K |
| **Starter** | 5-10% | Self-serve revenue | All core features, standard support | Basecamp: single plan $99/mo, no tiers |
| **Pro/Growth** | 3-5% | Expansion revenue | Advanced features, priority support | Linear: $8/seat, premium positioning |
| **Enterprise** | 1-2% | High-value accounts | SSO, audit, SLA, custom | "Call us" — 2-5x revenue per customer |

### Step 4: Price Raise Checklist

If you haven't raised prices in 6+ months:

- [ ] Raise by 20% → measure churn for 30 days
- [ ] If churn < 5% over baseline, raise again
- [ ] If churn spiked, test: was it price-sensitive segment or value gap?
- [ ] Grandfather existing customers at old price (no reason to churn)
- [ ] Add an annual plan (annual customers churn 60-70% less)

| Company | Price | Strategy | Outcome |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Linear** | $8/seat/mo vs Jira free/$7.75 | Premium over pain | $50M+ ARR |
| **Ahrefs** | $99-$999/mo | Value-based (SEO worth thousands) | $100M ARR |

| **DesignJoy** | $4,995/mo flat | Productized service premium | $1M ARR |
| **Buffer** | Landing page with pricing before building | Validated demand via price-click | $20M ARR |
| **Basecamp** | $99/mo single plan | Simplest possible pricing | $100M+ ARR |

## Pricing Traps (From Cemetery Data)

| Trap | What It Looks Like | Prevalence |
|---|---|---|
| **VC-subsidized CAC** | Spend $50 on ads to get $40 first order | Consumer #2 killer (114 cases) |
| **Free forever** | No conversion path, free users never pay | Common in failed consumer products |
| **Too cheap syndrome** | Nobody complains = too cheap | Chronic in technical founder products |
| **Competition-based pricing** | "Our competitor charges $X so we charge $X-1" | Ignores differentiation |
| **Enterprise too early** | $50K+ ACV before PMF at small scale | Kills focus and runway |
